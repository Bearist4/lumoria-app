Voice options for onboarding copy

3 directions with different dimension positions. Same screens, different voices. Prior "sensory/literary" direction rejected. Specimens shown in context.

A

Direct & plain

Casual · neutral · simple · authoritative. No flourish. Fastest to read. Says what it does.

B

Friendly coach

Casual · slightly playful · simple · friendly. Conversational. Encouraging but tight.

C

Instructional

Slightly formal · serious · simple · authoritative. Numbered steps. Task-first. No small talk.

My take

A (Direct) reads fastest and respects power users. Drops all poetry. Clearest CTA. Risk: feels a little generic. B (Friendly coach) still warm but drops the literary load. Good for first-time users who need a little encouragement. Risk: "Let's" can wear thin. C (Instructional) is the most explicit but feels like a tutorial, not a product. Matches existing UI verbs ("Export"). Risk: cold, overly procedural. Recommend A as baseline, borrowing B's "Maybe later" skip if you want the dismissal less blunt.
