# Guides

Short how-tos to help you get the most out of Lumoria. Every ticket is a moment — craft it, keep it, share it.

---

## How to create a ticket

Every Lumoria ticket starts as a blank canvas. Pick a kind, choose a look, fill in the details — done.

**1. Tap the star to start.**
From the home screen, tap the **+** to open the ticket funnel.

**2. Choose the category.**
Pick **Plane** or **Train**. (More kinds are on their way.)

**3. Choose the template.**
Each category has its own set of designs — Afterglow, Studio, Terminal, Heritage, Prism for planes; Express, Orient, Night for trains. Every template has its own personality. Pick the one that matches the moment.

**4. Choose the orientation.**
Vertical for a classic stub. Horizontal for a boarding-pass feel.

**5. Fill in the details.**
- **Planes:** airline, flight number, aircraft, cabin, origin and destination, date, time, gate, seat, terminal.
- **Trains:** origin city, destination city, date, time, car, seat, ticket number — plus a few template-specific fields (train type, passenger name, berth).

Every field is optional. Fill in only what matters to you.

**6. Style it.**
Some templates come with palette variants — dark, warm, muted. Try a few. The preview updates instantly.

**7. Save it forever.**
Tap **Save**. Watch it slide into your collection. It's yours now.

## How to import a ticket from Apple Wallet

If your boarding pass is already in Apple Wallet, Lumoria can read it and fill in the details for you.

**1. Open the pass.**
In **Wallet**, **Mail**, **Files**, or anywhere a `.pkpass` lives, tap the share icon.

**2. Send it to Lumoria.**
Scroll the share sheet and tap **Lumoria — Import to Lumoria**. You'll see *Pass saved — Open Lumoria* and the sheet will close on its own.

**3. Open Lumoria.**
The app picks up where Wallet left off. You'll land on **Drop in a boarding pass** with your pass already in hand — Lumoria reads it, matches the fields, and moves you straight to the form.

**4. Choose a design.**
Pick a template, pick a style. The flight or journey details are already filled in.

**5. Save it forever.**
Tap **Save**.

*If something doesn't read cleanly, tap **Fill manually** — Lumoria will keep whatever it managed to pull, and you can finish the rest by hand.*

## How to browse your collection

Every ticket you save lives in **All tickets** — your full gallery of moments.

**1. Open All tickets.**
Tap **All tickets** from the home screen.

**2. Scroll through your memories.**
Tickets arrange themselves into a two-column gallery. Vertical stubs pair up side by side; horizontal boarding passes take the full width. Tap any ticket to open it.

**3. Sort them.**
Tap the **sort icon** in the top corner and pick one:
- **Sort by date** — groups memories into Today, This week, This month, This year, and by year.
- **Sort by category** — groups them by kind (planes together, trains together).
- **Remove sorting** — goes back to the way you saved them.

A small dot on the sort icon tells you when a sort is active.

**4. Pull to refresh.**
Drag the gallery down to resync with the cloud. Anything you've made on another device shows up right here.

*If your gallery is still empty, you'll see the seven-point star and a gentle nudge — your gallery starts with one tap.*

## How to edit a ticket

Wrote the seat number wrong? Mistyped a gate? No problem. Every ticket stays editable forever.

**1. Open the ticket.**
From your collection, tap the one you want to change.

**2. Open the menu.**
Tap the **ellipsis (•••)** in the top corner and choose **Edit**.

**3. Change what you need.**
You'll land back in the ticket funnel with every field already filled in. Update anything — airline, date, seat, gate, passenger name, orientation. Every change previews live.

**4. Save the changes.**
Tap **Done**. Your ticket updates everywhere it lives — your gallery, your iMessage sticker pack, your memories.

*Note: the template and style stay locked to keep the design consistent. If you want a different look for the same journey, save a new ticket instead.*

## How to delete a ticket

Some memories you keep, some you let go. Lumoria won't stop you.

**1. Open the ticket.**
Tap it from your collection.

**2. Open the menu.**
Tap the **ellipsis (•••)** in the top corner.

**3. Tap "Delete ticket…".**

**4. Confirm.**
Lumoria will ask once — *Delete ticket* or *Keep ticket*. Tap **Delete ticket** to let it go.

The ticket disappears from your collection, your memories, and your iMessage sticker pack. This one is permanent — there's no undo, so choose with care.

## How to save a ticket to your Camera Roll

Lumoria makes export beautiful. Every ticket can leave the app as a polished image, ready for your camera roll, your lock screen, or anywhere you want to keep it.

**1. Open the ticket and tap Export.**
From any ticket, tap the **ellipsis (•••)** and choose **Export…**.

**2. Tap Camera roll.**

**3. Dial in the look.**
- **Format** — **PNG** keeps every edge crisp and preserves transparency; **JPG** makes a smaller file, ready to share.
- **Crop** — **Full** keeps the ticket's natural shape; **Square** centers it for a 1:1 grid-friendly frame.
- **Resolution** — **1x** for quick use, **2x** for a clean image, **3x** for anything you might want to print or blow up.
- **Background gradient** — on for a warm yellow-to-pink wash behind the ticket; off for a clean surface.
- **Watermark** — keep the quiet *Lumoria* mark, or turn it off. Your call.

The preview updates as you adjust. What you see is what saves.

**4. Tap Export.**
The first time, iOS will ask for permission to add to your Photos — allow it. You'll see *Saved to Camera roll.* and the image is yours.

*Tip: a 3x PNG with gradient off is the sharpest cutout for Stories, wallpapers, and further editing.*

## How to share a ticket via Messages, WhatsApp, or any app

Sometimes the moment is worth sharing with one person. Lumoria makes it one tap.

**1. Open the ticket and tap Export.**
Tap the **ellipsis (•••)**, then **Export…**.

**2. Tap Instant messaging.**

**3. Choose the app.**
iOS brings up the share sheet. Tap **Messages**, **WhatsApp**, **Telegram**, **Signal**, **Messenger**, **Discord** — whichever one the moment belongs in.

**4. Send it.**
The ticket goes as a high-resolution image, alongside a short message you can edit before sending.

No setup, no configuration. One tap, one share.

## How to use a ticket as a sticker in iMessage

Every ticket you make in Lumoria becomes an iMessage sticker, automatically. No extra step, no export.

**1. Open Messages.**
Tap into any conversation.

**2. Open the sticker drawer.**
Tap the **+** next to the text field, then tap **Stickers**. Swipe across to find the **Lumoria** pack.

**3. Send the moment.**
Tap any ticket to drop it into the thread. Tap and hold to place it on top of another message.

*First time using it? Open Lumoria at least once and save a ticket — that's what fills the sticker pack. Delete a ticket in Lumoria and it leaves the pack too.*

## How to use a ticket as a cutout on Instagram

Tickets make beautiful story stickers. Here's how to get one onto Instagram as a transparent cutout.

**1. Save the ticket to your camera roll.**
Follow the [Save a ticket to your Camera Roll](#how-to-save-a-ticket-to-your-camera-roll) guide, with:
- **Format:** PNG
- **Crop:** Full
- **Resolution:** 3x
- **Background gradient:** off
- **Watermark:** your call

**2. Open Instagram Stories.**
Tap the **+** at the top of your feed, then choose **Story**.

**3. Add the ticket as a sticker.**
Tap the sticker icon, tap **Cutout**, and pick your ticket from the camera roll. Instagram lifts the ticket off the background and turns it into a sticker you can resize, rotate, and place anywhere.

**4. Share it.**
Drop a backdrop, add a song, post.

*Tip: the Studio and Heritage templates cut out especially cleanly.*
